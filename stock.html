Addressing the Assumptions in Apple's "Stocks" Application

This is a problem that I personally saw and wanted to find a solution for.

The Problem

Stocks aren’t friendly to a lot of people the disconnect between women and wall street runs deep Investing seems scary and unapproachable: Words use acronyms Black seems and neon color Defying the Assumptions that the “Stock” App makes

Breaking down the Assumptions

Assumptions: Companies have acronyms Green means good, how good? Red means down, how bad is that? You can watch companies 1 day to 5 years News articles are important to inform stock prices Stocks are best presented in alphabetical Open, High, Low, Vol, P/E, Mkt Cap…. Etc Price at close, price after hours Do the graph or the numbers matter more? That the closing and after hours prices matter to be at the top That the sliding across at the top is useful? It only appears when you click on a company and then how long are you on a company —- not long enough to have any focus on anything else but the company, you came to the company for a reason…..
